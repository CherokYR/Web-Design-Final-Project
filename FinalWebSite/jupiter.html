Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in our solar system—a gas giant so massive that it contains more than twice the mass of all the other planets combined. It is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, similar to a star, but it never gained enough mass to ignite.
Key Characteristics:
The Great Red Spot: A gargantuan, hurricane-like storm larger than Earth that has been raging for centuries.
Rapid Rotation: Despite its size, Jupiter has the shortest day in the solar system, completing one rotation in about 9.9 hours.
Extreme Atmosphere: The planet is famous for its distinctive colorful bands and swirls, which are actually cold, windy clouds of ammonia and water.
Powerful Magnetosphere: It possesses the strongest magnetic field of any planet, creating intense radiation belts and brilliant auroras at its poles.Learn more
