Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun, orbiting at an average distance of 36 million miles (58 million km) every 88 Earth days. It is a rocky, cratered world with no moons, a massive iron core, and a thin exosphere. Due to its lack of atmosphere, it experiences extreme temperatures ranging from -180°C (-292°F) at night to 430°C (806°F) during the day.
Key Characteristics:
Size: The smallest planet, just slightly larger than Earth's Moon.
Structure: Terrestrial planet with a large metallic core (roughly 85% of its radius) that has caused it to shrink, creating large surface cliffs called "scarps".
Orbit & Rotation: High eccentricity orbit (46-70 million km) with a 3:2 spin-orbit resonance, meaning it rotates three times for every two revolutions.
Day/Night: A day-night cycle (one sunrise to the next) takes 176 Earth days.
