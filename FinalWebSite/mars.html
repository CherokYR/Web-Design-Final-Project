Mars

Mars, known as the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun and a cold, desert-like world with a very thin atmosphere. Its distinct reddish hue comes from the rusting of iron-rich minerals on its surface.
Key Physical Facts:
Size and Gravity: Mars is about half the size of Earth in diameter. Its gravity is roughly 38% of Earth's, meaning you could jump nearly three times as high there.
Time: A day on Mars (called a "sol") is slightly longer than an Earth day at about 24 hours and 37 minutes. However, a Martian year is nearly twice as long as Earth's, lasting 687 days.
Atmosphere: The atmosphere is 100 times thinner than Earth's and consists of 95% carbon dioxide, making it unbreathable for humans without life support.
