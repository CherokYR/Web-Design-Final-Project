Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and Earth’s closest planetary neighbor, often called Earth's "twin" due to its similar size, mass, and rocky composition. Despite these similarities, Venus is a hellish world with a surface temperature hot enough to melt lead (>460°C), driven by a thick, toxic carbon dioxide atmosphere that creates a runaway greenhouse effect. Here are key facts about Venus:
Hostile Environment: Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system, with surface temperatures averaging around 462°C.
Atmosphere & Pressure: The atmosphere is 96.5% carbon dioxide, with surface pressure about 90 times greater than Earth's—similar to the pressure found 3,000 feet deep in Earth's oceans.
Unique Rotation: Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets (retrograde rotation) and very slowly, with a day (243 Earth days) lasting longer than its year (225 Earth days).
