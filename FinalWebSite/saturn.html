Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system, renowned for its extensive, bright ring system made of ice and rock. As a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, it has no solid surface and is less dense than water. It boasts 146+ moons and a unique hexagonal storm at its north pole.
Key Facts & Characteristics:
Size: Over 9 times wider than Earth and 95 times more massive.
Rings: Seven main ring groups composed of billions of chunks of ice and rock.
Day/Year: A day is roughly 10.5 hours; it takes 29 Earth years to orbit the Sun.
Atmosphere: Extremely windy (up to 1,800 km/h) with a unique, persistent hexagonal storm at the north pole.
