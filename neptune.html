Neptune

Neptune is the most distant major planet in our solar system and is classified as an ice giant.
Key Characteristics:
Discovery: It was the first planet located using mathematical predictions (in 1846) rather than direct observation.
Appearance: It has a vibrant blue color caused by methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light and reflects blue.
Extreme Weather: It features the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).
Moons and Rings: Neptune has 16 known moons, including the large, backwards-orbiting Triton, and six faint rings.Learn more
