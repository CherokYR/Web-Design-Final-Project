Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, classified as an ice giant, and uniquely known for spinning on its side with a tilt. It has a faint, dark ring system, 29 confirmed moons, and a cold blue-green atmosphere of hydrogen, helium, and methane.
Key Facts About Uranus:
Ice Giant Composition: It is composed mostly of water, ammonia, and methane ices over a small rocky core. Its blue-green color arises from methane in its upper atmosphere.
Orbit and Rotation: It takes roughly 84 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun. A day on Uranus lasts about 17 hours and 14 minutes.
Extremely Cold: It is one of the coldest planets in the solar system, with cloudtop temperatures around -224°C (-371°F).
Ring System: Uranus has 13 known rings.
Moons: It has 29 confirmed moons, with the largest being Titania, Oberon, Umbriel, Ariel, and Miranda, each with unique geological features such as canyons, cliffs, and ice volcanoes.Learn more
