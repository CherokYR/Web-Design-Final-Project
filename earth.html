Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known place in the universe to support life. Formed about 4.5 billion years ago, it is a rocky (terrestrial) planet with a surface primarily covered by liquid water.
Key Facts & Figures:
Distance from Sun: Approximately 149.6 million km (93 million miles), known as 1 Astronomical Unit (AU).
Size: Fifth largest planet in the solar system; its diameter is roughly 12,756 km (7,926 miles).
Shape: An oblate spheroid—mostly round but bulging at the equator and slightly flattened at the poles due to its rotation.
Atmosphere: Composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases like argon and carbon dioxide.
Moon: Earth has one permanent natural satellite, the Moon, which helps stabilize the planet's axis and causes ocean tides.Learn more
